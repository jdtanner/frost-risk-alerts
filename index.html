Understanding the forecast
High risk — freezing air likely Medium risk — frost likely on clear, still night Low risk — some frost possible No risk — frost unlikely

Greenhouse buffer — an unheated greenhouse typically stays 2–5°C warmer than outside air on a calm, clear night. The buffer shrinks in windy conditions and on overcast nights (when outside temperatures stay higher anyway), and is slightly larger on clear nights when the outside air drops faster. The estimated minimum inside is adjusted for both wind and cloud cover. Use it to judge whether seedlings need extra protection such as fleece, bubble wrap lining, or a heated propagator.

Air frost — air temperature at 1.2 m drops to 0°C or below. Directly damaging to unprotected seedlings.

Ground frost — the ground surface reaches 0°C. Occurs when cold, still, clear-sky conditions allow the ground to radiate heat rapidly. Both wind and cloud cover significantly suppress ground frost risk — an overcast or breezy night at 2°C may see no ground frost at all.

Hoar frost — ice crystals form on surfaces when dewpoint falls below 0°C, wind is calm, and skies are clear. It is a purely radiative phenomenon and requires clear skies to form. Calculated from temperature, humidity, wind speed, and cloud cover.

Dewpoint — the temperature at which moisture in the air would condense. A sub-zero dewpoint indicates hoar frost potential.

Wind direction — shown as a compass bearing. In the UK, northerly and easterly winds (N, NE, E) tend to bring colder air than southwesterlies.

Cloud cover — clear skies allow the ground and air to radiate heat rapidly into space, significantly amplifying frost risk on borderline nights. Overcast conditions act as a thermal blanket and strongly suppress ground and hoar frost. Cloud cover is factored into the ground frost and hoar frost assessments above.

Rain chance — a high rain probability generally indicates cloud cover, which keeps surface temperatures up and suppresses radiation frost. Nights expecting rain are far less likely to see ground or hoar frost.
